Research Interests:

My research is in high-energy astrophysics, which provides unique and exciting laboratories mediated by strong gravity, plasma physics, and atomic physics. My research broadly studies compact object accretion and binaries by drawing upon insights from these areas. Currently, I'm working on:

- Quasi-Periodic Eruptions (QPEs), a newly discovered type of recurring X-ray transient which may be the first-known electromagnetic counterparts to EMRIs. An enticing possibility is using them to map general relativistic precession effects near supermassive black holes. See some illustrative animations of accretion disk nodal precession and EMRI apsidal precession (the "Q" in QPE).

- Ultracompact binaries, which are dominant low-frequency (mHz) gravitational wave sources in the galaxy and allow multi-messenger studies of accretion disks.

- Tidal Disruption Events, convenient laboratories to study rapid accretion and turbulence under strong gravity.

- High performance computing; large-scale data analysis; X-ray astronomy.
