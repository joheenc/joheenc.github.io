About Me

I'm a third-year physics PhD student at MIT working in high-energy astrophysics. My advisor is Erin Kara, and I also work closely with Kevin Burdge and Riccardo Arcodia.

I studied astrophysics and computer science as an undergrad at Columbia, where I worked with Frits Paerels on X-ray astronomy and David Kipping on extrasolar planets, graduating in 2022. Before that, I grew up in Edison, New Jersey.

Research Interests

High-energy astrophysics provides unique laboratories to study systems mediated by strong gravity, plasma physics, and atomic physics; my research broadly studies compact object accretion and binaries to uncover insights in these areas. Currently, I'm working on:

  • Quasi-Periodic Eruptions (QPEs), a newly discovered type of recurring X-ray transient which may be the first-known electromagnetic counterparts to EMRIs. An exciting possibility is that we may be able to use them to map the general relativistic precession down to ~hundreds of gravitational radii near supermassive black holes. Here are some illustrative animations showcasing the effects of accretion disk nodal precession and EMRI apsidal precession.
  • Ultracompact binaries, which are the dominant low-frequency (mHz) gravitational wave sources in the galaxy
  • Tidal Disruption Events, which are convenient laboratories to study rapid accretion and turbulence under strong gravity
  • High performance computing; large-scale data analysis; X-ray astronomy
